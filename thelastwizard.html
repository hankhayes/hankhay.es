    Background

    Gaming was a huge part of my childhood and has remained a hobby of mine. What interested me the most was how differently each development studio handled similar game mechanics. I wanted to learn more so I decided to take CS329E: Game Development.


    Software Development

    The class was designed to mimic a game development studio that was split into multiple groups. We developed our game over the course of 10 weeks using Agile methodologies and were able to get live feedback and conduct playtests during class. Below is a depction of the "home base" which was one of the first environments we completed.


    Challenges

    There was no shortage of challenges that arose throughout the semester. With no prior experience, my team and I had to learn the core principles of game devlopment as quickly as possible. We had to tackle physics, collision handling, object lifecycles, and much more.


    Result

    After 10 weeks, we had built a feature-rich game with a compelling story. The Last Wizard consists of 3 levels that challenge you to defeat all enemies. You can cast spells at our AI-driven enemies that chase you and even cast their own spells.
